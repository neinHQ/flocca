Flocca vs Runlayer: Why Flocca Is the Best Choice for Teams

If your goal is to help engineers ship faster from inside the IDE while keeping team controls practical, Flocca is built for that job.

High-level difference

Both products are in the MCP category, but they emphasize different starting points.

  • Runlayer positions itself around enterprise control-plane style governance.
  • Flocca supports both teams and enterprise, and focuses on day-to-day IDE execution for Dev, QA, DevOps, and PM teams with clear seat and access controls.

Why teams pick Flocca

1) Faster path to real work

Flocca is built around the workflow engineers already use. Teams connect tools and run ticket-to-code-to-test flows without context switching across multiple products.

2) Practical team access controls

Flocca supports role-based access with per-user SKU assignment. Owner/Admin users can assign access based on responsibilities, and one user can have multiple SKUs.

3) Predictable seat scaling

Teams start at 3 seats and Enterprise starts at 10 seats. Additional seats can be added when needed with immediate billing updates, which helps operations teams avoid procurement bottlenecks.

4) Broad connector coverage for delivery teams

Flocca supports the systems teams actually use together: issue tracking, source control, testing, CI/CD, cloud, and communication tools.

5) Transparent pricing from startup to enterprise scale

Flocca supports startups and large industries with transparent pricing. Teams starts at 3 seats, and Enterprise starts at 10 seats at $24.99 per seat/month. Additional seats are billed immediately and shown clearly in seat usage.

6) Enterprise controls without hidden pricing surprises

Flocca Enterprise includes role-based SKU assignment, multi-SKU users, Owner/Admin seat assignment, and clear top-up flows. Runlayer can involve hidden fees and less transparent pricing depending on contract structure.

Bottom line

For most engineering teams, the best platform is the one that reduces daily delivery friction while still giving admins enough control. Flocca is designed to do exactly that: connect tools quickly, manage access cleanly, and scale predictably as teams grow.
